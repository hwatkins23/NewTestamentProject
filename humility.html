Humility

Humility is a recurring theme throughout the New Testament, and it is exemplified in the life of Jesus Christ. He humbled himself by taking on human form and serving others, even to the point of sacrificing his life on t he cross. The Apostle Paul exhorts us to follow Christ's example and to "do nothing out of selfish ambition or vain conceit, but in humility consider others better than yourselves" (Philippians 2:3). The New Testament teaches that true greatness is found in serving others with a humble heart.

Christ's Example

One of the most powerful examples of Christ's humility in the New Testament is found in the account of the Last Supper. In John 13, Jesus is gathered with his disciples for the Passover meal, and he begins to wash their feet, a task normally reserved for servants. When Peter protests, Jesus responds, "Unless I wash you, you have no part with me" (John 13:8), emphasizing the importance of humility and service in following him. By washing the feet of his disciples, Jesus demonstrates his willingness to take on the role of a servant, despite being their teacher and leader. He humbly serves them, showing that true greatness is found in putting others first and serving them with love and compassion. This act of humility and service sets the tone for the rest of the Last Supper, and it serves as a powerful reminder of the kind of life Jesus calls his followers to live.
