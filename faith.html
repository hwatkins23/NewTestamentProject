Faith

According to the New Testament, faith is a confident trust in God's love and power, and it is essential for salvation. In the book of Hebrews, it is described as "the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen" (Hebrews 11:1). The New Testament emphasizes the importance of faith as a means of receiving God's grace and living a life pleasing to Him.

Christ's Example

There are many instances in the New Testament where Jesus demonstrated the principle of faith. One of the most well-known examples is found in the Gospel of Matthew, where Jesus walks on water. In Matthew 14:22-33, the disciples are in a boat on the Sea of Galilee when they see Jesus walking towards them on the water. Peter, one of the disciples, calls out to Jesus, saying, "Lord, if it's you, tell me to come to you on the water." Jesus responds, "Come," and Peter gets out of the boat and begins to walk towards Jesus on the water. However, as Peter becomes aware of the strong wind, he begins to fear and starts to sink. He cries out to Jesus for help, and Jesus reaches out his hand and catches him, saying, "You of little faith, why did you doubt?" (Matthew 14:31). In this powerful example, Jesus demonstrates the principle of faith, showing that it is possible to walk on water if one has faith and trust in God's power. However, when Peter's faith wavers, he starts to sink. This account serves as a reminder that faith is not just a mental exercise but involves putting one's trust into action, even in challenging circumstances.
